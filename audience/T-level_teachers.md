# Using Agentic Development to support delivery of T-Level courses

The approaches detailed in this repo and particularly in the [`examples/resource-gen/`](./examples/resource-gen/) directory are being used successfully to accelerate the creation of high-value learning resources for T-Level courses.

It's not a one-shot process or a "make me good resources, here's the spec, no mistakes, go" kind of thing - that still produces slop. But by applying your own expertise, experience and knowledge of your students to the agentic development process, you can get much better results than you would by doing it all yourself (if you could ever find the time), or by just asking an agent to do it all for you. What would have taken weeks or months to create, can now take a few afternoons of work.

Start with `examples/resource-gen/README.md` (along with the main repo `README.md`) for a detailed guide to how to use the repo, and the agents in `examples/resource-gen/agents/`.

Although the approaches here have been tested with T-Level courses, they should be adaptable to any course or subject, secondary, FE or beyond. The key is to have a clear and persistent set of instructions and rules for the agent(s) to follow (so it produces more consistent and reliable results), and a clear way to record what you're doing and what the state of the project/plan is. The rest is just scaffolding to help you do that.
